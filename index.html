How the numbers are worked out

Everything below uses published rest-of-UK rates for the tax year you select. Scotland has different income tax bands and is not yet covered.

Outside IR35 (limited company)

  1. Your company invoices the day rate across the weeks you work.
  2. It pays you a director's salary, plus employer NI on it (15% above £5,000; Employment Allowance does not apply to a single-director company).
  3. Corporation tax is charged on the profit left after salary, employer NI, pension and expenses: 19% up to £50,000, 25% above £250,000, with marginal relief between.
  4. The remaining profit is taken as dividends, taxed at 10.75%, 35.75% and 39.35% for 2026/27 (8.75%, 33.75% and 39.35% for 2025/26) after the £500 dividend allowance.
  5. Income tax and employee NI apply to the salary.

Inside IR35 (umbrella)

  1. The agency pays the umbrella your assignment rate.
  2. The umbrella keeps its margin, then deducts the employment costs it is liable for: employer NI (15% above £5,000) and, usually, the apprenticeship levy (0.5%).
  3. What is left becomes your gross pay.
  4. Income tax and employee NI are deducted from that gross pay, the same as any employee.
  5. You receive the rest. The employer costs are why an umbrella rate keeps less than the same headline rate outside IR35.
Rates used (with sources)
  • Personal allowance £12,570, tapered by £1 for every £2 of income over £100,000.
  • Income tax 20% to £50,270, 40% to £125,140, 45% above.
  • Employee NI 8% between £12,570 and £50,270, 2% above.
  • Employer NI 15% above the £5,000 secondary threshold.
  • Dividends £500 allowance, then 10.75% / 35.75% / 39.35% (2026/27); 8.75% / 33.75% / 39.35% (2025/26).
  • Corporation tax 19% to £50,000, 25% above £250,000, marginal relief (3/200) between.
  • Apprenticeship levy 0.5%.
  • Student loan 9% above the plan threshold (2026/27: Plan 1 £26,900, Plan 2 £29,385, Plan 4 £33,795, Plan 5 £25,000), Postgraduate 6% above £21,000.

Sources: HMRC rates and thresholds for employers, House of Commons Library briefings, gov.uk student loan repayment guidance for the selected year, and the Autumn 2025 Budget for the 2026/27 dividend rate rise.

Common questions

What is IR35, in one sentence?

IR35 is the rule that decides whether a contractor is, for tax purposes, really an employee of the client. If a role is "inside" IR35 you are taxed broadly like an employee; if it is "outside" you can work through your own company and take dividends.

What changed for the 2026/27 tax year?

The dividend tax rates rose by two percentage points from 6 April 2026: the ordinary rate went from 8.75% to 10.75% and the upper rate from 33.75% to 35.75% (the additional rate stays 39.35%). Everything else, income tax bands, National Insurance, the Employment Allowance and corporation tax, is unchanged from 2025/26. The dividend rise slightly narrows the outside-IR35 advantage, which you can see by switching the tax-year toggle.

Why do umbrella workers pay employer's National Insurance?

The rate quoted to an umbrella, the assignment rate, is not a salary. It has to cover the employment costs an employer normally pays, including employer NI and the apprenticeship levy. Those come out of the assignment rate before your gross pay is set, which is why the take-home looks lower than the headline figure.

Is outside IR35 always better paid?

At most day rates a limited company keeps more, because dividends avoid National Insurance and the company controls when profit is drawn. The gap narrows at lower rates, once you add accountancy fees and the admin of running a company, and again with the 2026/27 dividend rise. Inside-IR35 roles also sometimes carry a higher headline rate to compensate, which you can model above.

Does this include VAT?

No. For most contractors VAT is collected from the client and passed to HMRC, so it does not change take-home. The Flat Rate Scheme can leave a small surplus, but it is marginal and is left out to keep the comparison honest.

How accurate is it?

It uses published rates for the year you choose and the standard order of deductions, and the engine is unit-tested against hand-worked examples. It is a planning estimate, not tax advice, and it does not capture every personal circumstance. Check anything important with a qualified accountant.
